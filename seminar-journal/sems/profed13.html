Merry Metrics: Unwrapping the Presents of Statistics and SPSS

Insight no. 12

This seminar entitled "Merry Metrics: Unwrapping the Presents of Statistics and SPSS" is a combination of fun and knowledge. As a current 4th year student, statistics is a relevant subject for me because we are currently taking our Thesis Writing wherein the use of statistical tools are crucial. Our guest speaker, Prof. Christopher H. Punzalan, gave a simple yet very intricate discussion on the different statistical tools that we can use in our research paper.

The discussion started with an activity wherein we are asked to give possible studies that can be performed based only on one picture which shows a highway full of cars. Our guest speaker also reminded us on the different aspects of research which includes classifications of quantitative data and was followed by the different statistical tools that can be applied to various data types. I can sense that the Madlang Edukados are very attentive and active, even more than usual, in listening as well as in asking questions regarding their own research papers.

Afterwards, with the spirit of Christmas, some of the talented future educators gave us a little performance for the holidays. We are very busy with our school works lately and honestly, the spirit of Christmas and New Year is far from our priorities. It was a change in the atmosphere during the past seminars, but it was a good change. We had a lot of fun and it was refreshing.

Overall, it was an extraordinary experience and I did learn a lot from our guest speaker. The little performance at the end also provided us a space to breathe and just be there at the moment. As this was our last seminar, I know that I learned a lot from each of the seminar I attended and for that, I am grateful and thankful for the opportunity to experience the privilege of hearing great discussions from those who are experienced in the field of teaching.
